Gnocchi

Gnocchi are fluffy Italian dumplings made from flour, eggs and potatoes and served with a brown butter sauce

Fresh hand made gnocchi

Ingredients

  • 3 medium russet potatoes, scrubbed
  • 1.5 cups of all-purpose flour, and more for dusting
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 teaspoon of kosher salt

Instructions

  1. Gather the ingredients
  2. Place potatoes in a large saucepan and cover with 2 inches of salted water. Bring water to a boil over a high head; reduce the heat to a medium-high and let simmer undisturbed until potatoes are tender but still firm, roughly 25 to 30 minutes.
  3. Drain the water from the saucepan and let the potatoes dry and cool for 5 to 10 minutes
  4. Once the potatoes are cool enough to handle, peel potatoes and pass the peeled potatoes through a ricer into a large bowl
  5. Fold in flour, egg, and salt until flour is incorporated and dough starts to hold together. Transfer dough to a lightly floured surface and gently knead once or twice until mostly smooth (DO NOT overwork)
  6. Divide dough into 4 even portions. Roll and shape each of the portions into long tubes about 3/4 inch in diameter.
  7. Cut the tubes into 1/2 inch long pieces
  8. Bring a large bot of lightly salted water to a boil over high heat. Drop the gnocchi in and cook for 3 to 4 minutes until the gnocchi have risen to the top. Transfer cooked gnocchi to the desired sauce.
  9. Serve and Enjoy!
